What I changed my mind on

I started this with 18 single-stock picks. That was closet-indexing. Eighteen names is too many for a personal portfolio — you can't track that many catalysts, you can't size meaningfully, and the bottom 6 contribute noise instead of signal.

v2 is 10 positions. Eight singles where the moat is name-specific (TSM, LLY, MSFT, V, CEG, ETN, ANET, VRTX) and two ETFs where breadth wins (ITA for defense primes, SMH for the semi complex). Top 5 = 57% of the book. Concentrated. Convicted.

The pattern I'm watching: every time I added a pick to v1 it was because I felt bad excluding it, not because the marginal allocation moved the portfolio thesis. That's a tell. If the marginal pick doesn't change the story, it doesn't belong.

The Leopold curveball

Leopold Aschenbrenner's Q1 2026 13F filed May 18. The fund is Situational Awareness LP — $13.68B AUM, master-feeder with a sister entity. Two things to know.

One: his actual longs are second-derivative bets — memory (SanDisk), behind-the-meter power (Bloom Energy), GPU-cloud-not-NVDA (CoreWeave), data-center real-estate (Applied Digital), and bitcoin miners pivoting to AI HPC (IREN, CORZ, RIOT). Zero hyperscalers. Zero defense AI. Zero utility-scale power. He went on-site fuel cell over grid-scale nuclear.

Two — the part nobody is talking about: he loaded up on puts. $8.5B in put options across SMH ETF ($2B), NVIDIA ($1.57B), Oracle ($1.07B), Broadcom ($1.01B), AMD ($969M), plus MU/TSM/ASML/INTC/Corning. Every put is NEW in Q1. Zero puts in his Q4 2025 filing.

Translation: the man who authored the "compute-thesis-as-AGI" essay in June 2024 is now hedging the consensus version of his own thesis. He's saying it's priced in. The alpha left is in the second-derivatives, not the names everyone already owns.

For my book, this doesn't change the 3-5 year picks (longer horizon, different question). But it tells me to be careful with NVDA-heavy exposure. SMH ETF is my way of holding the semi complex without overweighting NVDA — and even that I sized at 8%, not 15%.

The hardest move in markets isn't picking. It's resizing positions when consensus catches up to your own thesis. Watch what the smartest LP actually does, not what their last essay said.

Anthropic IPO — not July

People keep asking. NOT July IPO. July is the rumored S-1 filing window. Actual IPO target: Q4 2026, October at earliest. Metaculus prediction market puts 66% odds of S-1 filing before July 1. The company's own spokesperson said "not decided when or even if it will go public."

Last official valuation: $380B post-money (Feb 2026 Series G). Secondary market: implied past $1T per Octagon AI. Revenue: $19B annualized as of March 2026, up from $9B in January. That's the most aggressive lab revenue curve in tech history.

How to get exposure now if you really want it:

  • Forge / EquityZen secondary: accredited investors, $50-100K minimums, 2-3× last round, lockup till IPO + 6 months
  • Public proxies: AMZN ($8B invested + AWS primary cloud) is the closest. GOOGL also (~$3B)
  • This portfolio's adjacent bet: MSFT (11% allocation) — different lab (OpenAI), same thesis
  • Wait: day-1 post-IPO is usually the worst entry. Lock-up expiry + 6 months is when insiders unload — buy then
