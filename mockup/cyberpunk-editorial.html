What changed & why

/ 01 · TYPOGRAPHY
Newsreader serif + Space Grotesk + JetBrains Mono
Display swapped from Fraunces → Newsreader — more editorial, cleaner italic, less luxurious. Body from Instrument Sans → Space Grotesk — geometric edge, cyberpunk-adjacent without being cyber-cliché. Mono stays JetBrains Mono — terminal authenticity. Hero uses serif italic for emphasis, mono for numbers — magazine page rhythm.
/ 02 · COLOR — TWO-ACCENT SYSTEM
Sodium-vapor amber + electric cyan
Primary accent: amber #FFB000 (sodium-vapor lamp, Bloomberg terminal). Used everywhere — buttons, borders, ticker symbols. Pulse accent: cyan #00E5FF — used sparingly only for LIVE indicators, the brand dot, "tracked" badges. The rarity is what makes it punch. Avoid: purple gradients, matrix green, AI-slop neon explosions.
/ 03 · LIGHT MODE
Warm newsprint cream + bronze + deep teal
Light mode is NOT just inverted dark. Amber softens to bronze #C25E00 for proper contrast on cream paper. Cyan pulse becomes deep teal #00798F. Scan-line texture removed entirely (white space breathes; scans on paper feel wrong). Background = warm cream #F5F1E6, not bleached white. Newspaper aesthetic.

Toggle the theme at top-right to see the side-by-side.
/ 04 · TEXTURE — RESTRAINED
Scan lines at .04 opacity, only on dark
Most cyberpunk designs over-do scan lines, glitch animations, neon glows. Here: scan lines barely visible (4% opacity), no animation, no glitch effects on hover. The only animation is the LIVE dot pulse — once every 1.8 seconds. Restraint = editorial polish. The cyberpunk language is in the typography, layout grid, and accent placement — not in motion design.
/ 05 · LAYOUT — DENSE GRID
Hairline 1px borders, no rounded corners
Every card has sharp 1px borders. Zero border-radius. Grid cells share borders for tight density (the picks row + news grid use a 1px gap with background:var(--line) trick). Magazine pages don't have rounded corners. Terminal interfaces don't either. Combining the two = no rounding.
/ 06 · WHAT TO DECIDE
Three open questions
① Accent color — keep amber+cyan, or switch primary to magenta / lime / kept-from-v1 warm gold? ② Hero treatment — current is large serif italic. Alternative = all-mono with rotating headlines. ③ Pick card weight — current is moderate density. Alternative = ultra-dense table-row treatment. Reply and I'll spin a v2 variant on whichever direction wins.
