Sources + cadence

13F filings

SEC EDGAR · quarterly

  • Filer: any institution managing >$100M in 13F-eligible US securities must file
  • Cadence: 45 days after quarter end (Feb 14, May 15, Aug 14, Nov 14)
  • What's in: long equity positions, calls + puts (notional), shares + value
  • What's NOT in: shorts, cash, foreign equity, fixed income — only US 13F-eligible
  • Source URL: efts.sec.gov/LATEST/search-index?q=&forms=13F-HR

Congressional disclosures

STOCK Act PTR · 45 days post-trade

  • Filer: all members of Congress + senior staff, per 2012 STOCK Act
  • Cadence: Periodic Transaction Report within 45 days of trade
  • What's in: ticker, transaction date, buy/sell, amount range (e.g. "$1K-$15K")
  • What's NOT in: exact dollar amounts, holdings totals (only annual disclosure has those)
  • Source URL: capitoltrades.com / quiverquant.com (parsed feeds)

Limitations to know

Read 13F as direction, not action

  • 45 days stale: By the time you see Q1 holdings, the fund may have rotated. Treat as directional signal.
  • No price detail: 13F shows shares + value as of period end. Cost basis unknown — you don't know if they bought at $50 or $500.
  • Puts notional only: Notional value visible, but not strike/expiration. Don't assume hedged or directional.
  • Selection bias: Famous investors get tracked because they're famous, not because their style fits your situation.
