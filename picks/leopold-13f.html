My take

For my own book — three rules

Rule 1

Don't mirror the puts

Retail can't replicate 13F puts efficiently. The strike + delta selection is bespoke. SQQQ or SOXS as a retail hedge just bleeds theta. If you want to express bearish view, just under-weight the put names — don't actively short them.

Rule 2

His top long is too rich

SanDisk +107% from his Q1 cost basis. The asymmetry is gone. Don't chase his #1 just because it's his #1. Look at WHEN he entered, not just WHAT he holds. The picks-and-shovels still cheaper than his average cost: BE (+87%), CRWV (+26%), IREN (+39%).

Rule 3

Use the signal, not the trade

The 13F is a snapshot 45 days old by the time you see it. He may have rotated. What's durable is the THESIS — he's signaling that within the AI complex, the second-derivatives compound faster than the consensus names from here. That informs the 3-5yr book by reducing my NVDA-direct exposure (held only as ~1.5% inside SMH).
