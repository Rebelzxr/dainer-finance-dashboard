02 · The tax math

For a Malaysian holder

The reason MY names get a 20% sleeve isn't patriotism — it's net yield math.

US dividends

30% withholding for Malaysia residents

  • Default: 30% US federal withholding on every dividend
  • W-8BEN: confirms foreign status, but Malaysia is not on the IRS US income tax treaty list
  • Effective net: 4% gross × 0.70 = 2.8% net
  • ETF distributions: same treatment as individual stocks
  • What to do: still file W-8BEN with IBKR / Charles Schwab / Saxo / whichever broker so the account is correctly documented.

MY dividends

Tax-free at receiver level

  • Single-tier system: dividend tax paid by company (corporate tax 24%); zero further tax on receiver
  • Effective net: 5% gross × 1.00 = 5% net
  • Comparison: 5% US gross becomes 4.25% net; 5% MY gross stays 5% net
  • Why MY ringgit sleeve exists: pure yield favours MY at equivalent gross yield
  • Offset: MYR depreciation vs USD over 5-10yr — see Risk section. The right mix balances yield-after-tax against FX

05 · What kills the income

Risks to the dividend sleeve

RISK 01

Rate spike

REITs (O, VNQ), telcos (VZ), utilities (ED, TENAGA) all re-rate down hard on a 100bp+ move in long rates. Combined that's ~30% of the income sleeve. Mitigation: BDCs (ARCC, MAIN) are floating-rate and rate-positive — they cushion.

RISK 02

Tax treaty change

US dividends already start from a 30% Malaysia-resident withholding assumption. Net yield is lower than most dividend screeners show. Mitigation: compare each buy against MY / SG / HK / UK income alternatives in the tax calculator.

RISK 03

Recession dividend cuts

High-yield Sleeve B names cut first. ARCC + MAIN credit losses spike in recession. ENB / KMI commodity exposure. VZ debt servicing. Mitigation: Sleeve A names have 25+yr no-cut streaks — even GFC didn't break JNJ, PG, KO.

RISK 04

Ringgit collapse

If MYR drops 20%+ vs USD (it has historically), Sleeve C income loses USD purchasing power. But MY assets gain in MYR terms — fine if MY-residing. Hurts only if planning to convert MY income to USD.

RISK 05

Yield trap individual names

Examples deliberately excluded: MMM (cut Apr 2024), WBA (cut Jan 2024), T (cut 2022), KHC (peaked structurally), MO (terminal asset thesis). High yield often signals underlying business weakness. Watch payout ratio + FCF coverage every quarter.

RISK 06

Bank Negara OPR cut (MY)

If BNM cuts OPR to support growth, MAYBANK + PBBANK NIM compresses → dividend coverage tightens. Both have 73-80% payout — limited buffer. Sleeve C is bank-heavy (3 of 7 names). Counterbalance: REITs (SUNREIT, PAVREIT) benefit from rate cuts.
