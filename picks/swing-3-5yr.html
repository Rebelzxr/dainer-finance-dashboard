Aside · the AI IPO question

Anthropic IPO — not July

The portfolio above is public-equity-only. But you asked about the AI lab itself — so here's the current state, as of 2026-05-20.

What's confirmed

No S-1 filed. No date. No ticker.

  • Series G: $380B post-money, closed Feb 2026 — last official valuation
  • Revenue: $19B annualized as of March 2026, up from $9B in January (per The Information)
  • Legal counsel: Wilson Sonsini Goodrich & Rosati hired for IPO prep (same firm that did Google, LinkedIn)
  • Secondary market: implied valuation past $1T (Octagon AI, May 2026 secondary trades)
  • Spokesperson quote: "not decided when or even if it will go public"

What's rumored (not confirmed)

S-1 in July · IPO in Q4 2026

  • July 2026: rumored confidential S-1 filing window — Metaculus prediction market puts 66% probability of S-1 before July 1
  • October 2026: earliest realistic public listing per The Information internal-executive reporting
  • Q4 2026: the internal target executives have discussed (per same source)
  • Reality check: Stripe, SpaceX, and OpenAI all "rumored Q4" for multiple years running. Confidential S-1 ≠ IPO. Anthropic could file and sit on it for 12+ months.

Pre-IPO exposure routes

Three ways to get there now

  • Forge / EquityZen secondary: accredited investors can buy private Anthropic shares — minimums $50-100K, valuations 2-3× last round, lockup until IPO + 6 months
  • Public-equity proxies: AMZN (invested $8B + AWS primary cloud) and GOOGL (invested ~$3B) are the closest tracker exposure. AMZN holds the biggest stake
  • This portfolio's proxy: MSFT (11% allocation) is the OpenAI-side bet — different lab, same thesis. Not a perfect Anthropic hedge but adjacent

What I'd actually do

Wait for the S-1

  • Day 1 post-IPO is usually the worst entry. Lock-up expiry +6 months is when insiders unload — buy then, not at listing
  • Watch the SEC EDGAR ticker: a confidential S-1 becomes public ~15 days before roadshow. That's the real timing signal
  • Don't pay 2-3× last round on secondary unless your time horizon is 5+ years AND you can tolerate the lock-up
  • Sizing rule for any single AI lab IPO: max 5% of the swing book post-IPO. Same goes for OpenAI / xAI / Mistral if any list 2026-2027

05 · What kills the basket

Things that void the thesis

Honest list. Some risks are common-mode — one event nukes multiple positions at once. Sized accordingly.

RISK 01

Taiwan kinetic event

TSM, ASML, AVGO, ANET, VRT — every AI-infra name is downstream of TSMC silicon. There's no diversifying away from this; you accept it by holding the thesis. Mitigation: position-size such that a 60% drawdown in 29% of book = ~17% portfolio loss, survivable.

RISK 02

Hyperscaler AI capex pause

A coordinated MSFT/META/AMZN/GOOGL guide-down in any quarter would correlate-crater the AI infra book. Watch their July + October earnings cycles. Mitigation: ETN, V, MSFT, PGR, LLY are less downstream of capex — they hold.

RISK 03

Keytruda 2028 LOE for MRK

Not in this basket. Listed because if you replicate from broader healthcare research, MRK is a value trap unless sub-Q Keytruda hits. The basket explicitly skips MRK — LLY/VRTX/ISRG/IDXX have their own moats that don't depend on patent extension.

RISK 04

Peace dividend

If Ukraine ceasefire holds + Middle East de-escalates + Taiwan stays cold, defense primes lose the urgency premium. GD/HII are capacity-constrained (4x backlog) so order books cushion 2-3yr. After that, base case. Sized at 12% for this reason.

RISK 05

Power buildout regulatory block

FERC or state PUCs (VA, TX, OH) move to ration grid interconnects to data centers or impose AI-tax — CEG/ETN/GEV re-rate hard. Watch Virginia legislature 2026 session. Mitigation: ETN's order book is 70%+ international.

RISK 06

Rate spike

100bp+ move higher in long rates compresses every quality compounder multiple. MSFT, V, ANET, VRTX trade at 22-37x because the discount rate is benign. Mitigation: ITA underlying primes (LMT, RTX, GD) are rate-positive cohorts that benefit from rising rate environment; tilt 2-3% from singles into ITA if 10yr breaks 5.5%.

06 · How to actually leg in

Execution is the alpha tax

Don't lump $100K today. Don't wait six months either. Most retail underperforms the strategy because of execution, not picks.

Leg-in plan

50 / 25 / 25 across 6 weeks

  • Week 1: Buy 50% notional across all 10 positions at market. Don't optimize entries on this tranche.
  • Week 3: Buy 25% more. If any name has dropped 8%+ since week 1, double the slice in that one.
  • Weeks 5-8: Buy remaining 25% on dips. If everything has rallied through week 8, accept slightly worse entries — being in the trade beats being clever about timing.
  • ETF shortcut: ITA + SMH can both be lump-summed week 1. They're diversified by construction — no single-name entry risk.

Rebalance triggers

Quarterly check, annual reset

  • Single position > 18%: trim 25% of excess back into laggards. This will mostly fire on TSM if AI keeps running.
  • Single position drops 25% peak-to-trough: if no thesis-breaking news, add. If catalyst (FDA fail, customer loss, fraud) → cut to zero.
  • Annual reset (May 2027): re-run the same 5-agent research. If any theme thesis has played out fully (rare) or fully broken (also rare) — rotate sleeve, not individual names.
  • Tax lots: long-term gains kick in at month 13. Don't trim winners pre-13mo unless thesis dies.

Instant exit signals

What forces a hard cut

  • TSM: Taiwan kinetic event (not Pelosi-style flyover — actual blockade or strike).
  • LLY: Phase 3 cardiovascular outcomes failure for orforglipron, OR GLP-1 supply normalizes faster than NVO/AMGN expected — kills pricing power.
  • MSFT: Azure AI growth decelerates two consecutive quarters with no acceleration in agentic / GitHub Copilot SKUs.
  • CEG: Three Mile Island re-start delayed 24+ months OR Microsoft PPA renegotiation downward.
  • VRTX: Casgevy commercial launch underperforms FY27 street by 30%+ AND pain platform (suzetrigine) misses primary endpoint.
  • ITA (ETF): Cut to half-weight if Ukraine ceasefire + Middle East formal de-escalation hit within 6 months. Don't fully exit — ETF diversification cushions.
  • SMH (ETF): Cut to half-weight if hyperscaler capex paused (MSFT + META + AMZN + GOOGL all guide down on same quarter). Don't fully exit — TSM and ASML inside SMH would survive.

What this report is NOT

Honest disclaimers

  • Not a Leopold-style trade: his Q1 2026 13F is 1-2 quarter horizon with $8.5B in puts. This basket has zero puts. Different beast.
  • Not a Buffett portfolio: Berkshire concentration is 8-9 names. This is 18 — wider for diversification across themes.
  • Not market-neutral: beta is ~1.1 to S&P. In a 2009 drawdown this likely down 35-45%. Survives, doesn't escape.
  • Not options-overlaid: no covered calls, no puts. Pure long equity. If you want to overlay, buy 3-month 5%-OTM puts on SPY equal to 8-10% of book — bleed theta but cushion tail.
