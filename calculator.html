02 · What the numbers say

The three lessons

Lesson 1

Time beats amount

  • 20 years at 8%: $1,000/mo → ~$590K. You contributed $240K.
  • 30 years at 8%: $1,000/mo → ~$1.49M. You contributed $360K.
  • The extra decade: 50% more cash in for 150% more value. The last 10 years compound the first 20.
  • Implication: start now beats start optimal.

Lesson 2

Return rate is non-linear

  • $500/mo for 30 yrs: 6% → $500K · 8% → $750K · 10% → $1.13M · 12% → $1.74M
  • 4% extra return = 3× the value. Not 4% extra. Three times.
  • What costs you 4% return: 1.5% advisor fee + 1% fund expense + tax drag + behavioural mistakes.
  • Implication: fees compound against you the same way returns compound for you.

Lesson 3

Monthly add matters more than first deposit

  • $50K start + $0/mo: at 8% × 30yr = $503K. Boring.
  • $0 start + $500/mo: at 8% × 30yr = $745K. Beats above.
  • $50K start + $500/mo: at 8% × 30yr = $1.25M. The combination compounds.
  • Implication: the monthly habit is what builds wealth. Lump-sum head-start helps but isn't decisive.

Realistic assumptions

What return rate to use

  • 6%: Treasury-heavy / bond-leaning conservative portfolio. Real after-inflation ~3%.
  • 8%: US S&P 500 long-run nominal average (1928-2024 = ~10%; recent decade higher; expect mean reversion). Use 8% for forward planning.
  • 10%: Active-managed equity with some alpha. Optimistic for retail without edge.
  • 12%+: Concentrated growth / individual-stock portfolio. Achievable for short windows; very hard to sustain 20+ years. Don't plan around this.
